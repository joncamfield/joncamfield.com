So what is SAFETAG?

SAFETAG is an organizational security auditing and assessment tool designed for the NGO community.

It currently exists as an auditor guide with 12 thematic areas and over 50 specific exercises tailored for small organizations with limited technical resources, with a strong focus on agency-building and organizational change dynamics throughout the process.
It has a corresponding curricula to train new auditors, and is available in English and Spanish.

Who is SAFETAG?

SAFETAG began and remains managed by Internews

SAFETAG is an open (CC) framework, and both welcomes and has contributions from practicitioners around the world.


Internews has used and adapted SAFETAG over its 5+ year life in dozens of audits ourselves, and it has been adopted in various forms by the ISC Project, Frontline Defenders, NDI, and many independent auditors as well.

Understanding Capacity and Building Agency

SAFETAG differs from other organizational risk assessment practices by understanding and focusing on each organization's ability to change and their current capacity to adopt more secure practices.
