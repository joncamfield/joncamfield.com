So what is SAFETAG?

SAFETAG is an organizational security auditing and assessment tool designed for the NGO community. It currently exists as an auditor guide with 12 thematic areas and over 50 specific exercises tailored for small organizations with limited technical resources, with a strong focus on agency-building and organizational change dynamics throughout the process. It has a corresponding curricula to train new auditors, and is available in English and Spanish. Internews has used and adapted SAFETAG over its 3+ year life in dozens of audits ourselves, and it has been adopted in various forms by the ISC Project, Frontline Defenders, NDI, and many independent auditors as well.

Methods and Activities

Methods are goal-oriented and answer a specific set of questions

Activities are specific actions one might take to help discover those answers

A Method for example could be around discovering what cookies people at the organization like. Activities to help answer that could include surveys, observation of which cookies go the fastest when left out in the kitchen, or tracking cookie purchasing data at the office.

Context Research

Understanding the context surrounding the organization and their work

Discovering legal limits and regulations impacting auditing approaches and recommendations

Researching and assessing potential threat actors

Capacity Assessment

Understanding the management structure

Identifying "champions" and key personnel

Initial exploration of existing capacity, organizational change approaches, and available resources

Pre-audit discovery of known organizational technology

Reconnaissance

Exploring how much information about the organization is "discoverable"

Identifying potentially forgotten assets (dev servers) or risks for social engineering attacks

Process Mapping and Risk Modeling

Deeply and collaborately mapping out organizational processes

Identifying points of risk and what their impacts could be

Prioritizing impacts to help identify what risks will need immediate mitigation based on the organization's risk tolerance

Physical and Operational Security

Surveying physical security of the office space

Identifying common practices of computer and data device usage/storage

Discussing travel procedures and local risks

Data Mapping

Collaboratively exploring where the organization stores data

Organizational Device Usage

Looking at servers, backup systems, and websites

Mapping out any office network infrastructure (wifi, security and reach, wired networks, network devices)

Vulnerability scanning of in-scope servers/devices

User Device Assessment

Working directly with users and how they interact with organizational technology

If in scope, also covering mobile and personal devices which are also used for work

Surveying device policies, updates, software licenses, and virus/malware resistance

Responding to Advanced Threats

More advanced practices if you have reason to suspect the organization is a victim of targeted malware

Focused on identification, triage, and incident response

Includes limited analysis and evidence capture instructions for when those are judged appropriate

Threat Assessment

Research and discussion of known threat actors, history of attacks, and potential triggers

Responsive Support

Don't fix things!

Ensure organization can receive and manage the audit report

Respond to critical needs

Debrief

Psycho-social care

Review of priorities and threats

Planning next steps

Follow Up and Reporting

Providing follow-up ineractions and a formal report on findings and threat/capacity-aware recommendations
